WELCOME TO ZIMBABWE

Zimbabwe is a landlocked nation in southern Africa that offers a unique blend of natural wonders, wildlife, and cultural heritage. The country is known for its majestic Victoria Falls, ancient ruins, and pristine national parks. Zimbabwe's capital city, Harare, provides a glimpse into the country's rich history and modern life. The country's diverse ecosystems support a wide range of wildlife, making it an ideal destination for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers.

SAFARI ADVENTURES

VICTORIA FALLS

Victoria Falls is located on the Zambezi River, forming the border between Zimbabwe to the south and Zambia to the north. The falls sit on a basalt plateau approximately 900 meters above sea level, with the river flowing over a level sheet of basalt before plunging into a deep chasm called the First Gorge, which varies in depth from 80 to 108 meters. The waterfall is divided into several main sections: Devil's Cataract, Main Falls, Rainbow Falls, and Eastern Cataract, with two islands—Livingstone Island and Boaruka (Cataract) Island—situated on the crest of the falls.

MANA POOLS NATIONAL PARK

Mana Pools National Park is a breathtaking safari destination known for its wild beauty and close wildlife encounters along the Zambezi River. Tourists can enjoy unique experiences like walking safaris and canoe trips while seeing elephants, hippos, and lions up close. Its peaceful, untouched environment makes it perfect for travelers seeking adventure and a true African wilderness experience.
